Neural Networks for Text Mining

Overview

This section explores the application of neural networks to text mining tasks, particularly in the context of analyzing climate change discourse. Neural networks provide powerful capabilities for understanding complex patterns in textual data that traditional methods might miss.

Neural Network Approaches

Word Embeddings

Using techniques like Word2Vec and GloVe to represent words as vectors in a continuous vector space, capturing semantic meanings and relationships.

Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs)

Applying sequence models like LSTM and GRU to capture contextual information in text documents for classification tasks.

Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)

Using CNNs to identify local patterns and features in text data through convolution operations.

Transformer Models

Leveraging attention mechanisms in transformer architectures to capture long-range dependencies in text.

Results & Applications

The neural network models developed in this project have been applied to:

  • Classification of climate change related content
  • Sentiment analysis of climate discourse
  • Topic modeling and discovery
  • Contextual understanding of climate terminology

These applications demonstrate the power of neural networks in understanding nuanced aspects of climate change communication across different platforms and communities.
